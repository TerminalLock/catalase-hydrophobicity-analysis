## About This Project
Axolotls are most well known for their exceptional ability to regenerate lost appendages. Because of this, Axolotls are the model species for studying cellular regeneration. 
Researchers from Yale have identified catalase one (CAT1) and fetuin-b as potential genes that facilitate limb regeneration as their absence in grafted mutants lagged behind non-mutants.<sup>[[1](https://elifesciences.org/articles/48511)]</sup>.
This analysis intends to investigates CAT1's structure by measuring the mean hydrophobicity across 9 species and see if differences may affect their regenerative capacity.
